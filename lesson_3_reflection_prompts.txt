When would you want to use a remote repository rather than keeping all your work local?

- When you have work that you'd like to share with others.

Why might you always want to pull changes manually rather than having GitHub stay up to date with your remote repository?

- So you can decide when you synch your changes.

Describe the differences between forks, clones and branches. When would you use one instead of another?

- Forks: For cloning something in GitHub. Gives credit to original author. Use when you want to spin off an open source project.
- Clone: For making a copy of something. No credit given unless you explicitly do it yourself.
- Branch: For making a version of something designed to be merged back into the master copy. Use when you want to fix a bug or experiment.
